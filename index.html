9 Most Devasating Earthquakes!

By: Glenneca Hall

Earthquake:

Valdivia, Chile (May 22, 1960)

Magnitude:

9.5

Epicenter:

25km (16mi)

Damages:

Lasted 10 minutes and created a tsunami; wrecking coastal communities. Leaving 3000 injured and 2 million homeless. The ecomic damage totaled $550 million.

# of Fatalities:

1,655 killed
Tohoku, Japan (March 11, 2011) 9.0-9.1 130km (81mi) Lasted 6 minutes leaving over 450,000 people homeless. Thousands of homes, businesses, roads, and railways were destoryed. Over 18,000 dead
Kamchatka Peninsula, Russia (November 4, 1952) 9 21.6 km (13mi) $17 million in damages include the damage to shorelines, infrastructures, and the destruction of small bridges. 10,000-15,000 dead
Sumatra, Indonesia (December 26, 2004) 9.1 30km (19mi) Around $10 million worth of damages. The earthquake turned into a tsunami that traveled as fast as 500mph. Many communities were lost. 230,000 people death
Ecuador-colombia (January 31, 1906) 8.8 20km 35,000 homes were destoryed leaving mnay familes to have to relocate. Over 700,000 people needed assistance and over 100,000 needed shelter. 500-1,5000 killed
Indian Ocean (December 26, 2004) 8.8 30km (18.6mi) Lasted 15 minutes displacing thousands, destroying towns, homes, livelihoods, infrastructure, and wrecked coastal areas. Over 275,000 killed
Haiti (January 12, 2010) 7.0 13km (8.1mi) 4,000 schools were damaged or destroyed and over 600,000 people left their home areo. 1.5 million people homeless and 19 million cubic metres of rubble and debris left behind. Over 200,000 deaths
Assam-tibet (August 15, 1950) 8.6 15km (9.3mi) 70 villages destoryed. Large landslides blocked the Subansiri River. 8 days after the natural dam broke; killing 536 more people. 789 killed
Lisbon (November 1, 1755) 8.5 21km A series of earthquakes that caused damages to the port city of Lisbon. Many worshippers dies or were injured in churches that could not withstand the shocks, and collapsed. About 60,000 killed
